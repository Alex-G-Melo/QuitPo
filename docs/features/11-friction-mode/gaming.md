## Recommended Apps for Friction

### Critical (Consider Deleting)
| App | Reason |
|-----|--------|
| Steam | Direct game access |
| Epic Games | Direct game access |
| Xbox app | Game access, social |
| PlayStation app | Game access, social |
| Any mobile games | Direct play access |

**Note**: For gaming addiction, game launcher apps should ideally be deleted. Friction is a secondary defense.

| App | Reason |
|-----|--------|

| App | Reason |

## Addiction-Specific Messages

### Real Life Messages
```
"Real life has quests too. And the rewards
are actually real."

"What skill could you level up in real life
with the time you'd spend gaming?"

"Virtual achievements don't follow you
when you close the app. Real ones do."

"The world outside is the best graphics
you'll ever see."
```

### Time Messages
```
"One game leads to eight hours.
You know how this goes."

"How many hours have you already given to games?
Are you okay giving more?"

"That time could be spent on something
that makes your actual life better."
```

### Social Pressure Messages
```
"Your guild will survive without you.
Your real life needs you more."

"FOMO for virtual events isn't worth it.
Real experiences are waiting."

"The game will still exist in 90 days.
Play it then if you still want to."
```

### Health Messages
```
"Your eyes, your posture, your sleep
all suffer from more gaming."

"How's your body feeling? Gaming won't
make it feel better."

"Real physical activity would serve you
better right now."
```

## Pattern Detection

### High-Risk Pattern Recognition
```
If friction triggers for Twitch/YouTube gaming:
→ "Watching gaming content keeps games in your head.
   It's like an alcoholic hanging out in bars."

If friction triggers on game release day:
→ "Release day is the hardest. This feeling will
   pass in a few days. You can do this."

If friction triggers during old raid/play times:
→ "This is when you used to [raid/play]. Old habits
   are pulling at you. The schedule doesn't own you."
```

### Trigger Chain Detection
```
Pattern detected:
YouTube gaming → Discord → Friction on Steam

Insight: "Gaming content leads you toward playing.
Consider adding friction to YouTube and Discord too,
or blocking gaming content entirely."
```

## Discord-Specific Handling

### Guild/Friend Activity Detection
```
If Discord friction triggers during peak guild times:
→ "Your guild is probably online right now.
   It's okay to miss sessions. Real life matters more."

→ "The FOMO of missing out on what your friends
   are doing is temporary. Recovery is permanent."
```

### Recommendation
```
"For many gamers, Discord is the hardest
to step away from because of social ties.

Consider:
- Leaving gaming servers (you can rejoin later)
- Messaging friends you want to keep
- Muting all gaming server notifications"
```

## Console/PC Gaming Notes

### Mobile App as Gateway
```
"Mobile gaming apps keep you connected
to your gaming identity and trigger urges.

Steam, Epic, Xbox, and PlayStation apps
should ideally be deleted entirely, not
just given friction.

If you must keep them, friction is your
last line of defense."
```

### After Deflection: Physical Action
```
If user deflects from gaming app:

"Great choice. To really break the pull,
consider a physical action:

- Put your phone in another room
- Go outside for even 5 minutes
- Do 10 push-ups

Physical action helps reset your brain
more than just closing the app."
```
