# Social Media Safety - Social Media/Phone Addiction

This file contains social media addiction-specific platform safety configurations. See [README.md](README.md) for shared functionality.

---

## Focus

Configure platforms to reduce:
- Infinite scroll engagement
- Algorithmic manipulation
- Notification spam
- FOMO-inducing features
- Comparison and envy triggers
- Addictive feature mechanics (streaks, likes, etc.)

---

## Platform Guides

### Instagram Safety Guide (8 Steps)

**Step 1: Disable Likes Visibility**
1. Settings → Privacy
2. Posts → Hide like and view counts
3. This reduces comparison/validation seeking

**Step 2: Limit Notifications**
1. Settings → Notifications
2. Turn OFF: Likes, Comments from everyone
3. Keep only: DMs from friends, mentions
4. Turn OFF: Suggested posts, IGTV, etc.

**Step 3: Remove Explore Tab Appeal**
1. Settings → Suggested content → Sensitive content → Less
2. Clear recommendation history
3. Consider not using Explore at all

**Step 4: Time Management**
1. Settings → Your activity → Time spent
2. Set daily reminder at your limit (e.g., 30 min)
3. Turn ON "Quiet mode" during work/sleep hours

**Step 5: Reduce Story FOMO**
1. Mute stories from most accounts
2. Keep only close friends unmuted
3. Consider hiding your story views count

**Step 6: Clean Your Feed**
1. Unfollow accounts that make you feel bad
2. Mute accounts you don't want to unfollow
3. Keep only accounts that add value

**Step 7: Disable Reels Autoplay**
1. Settings → Account → Data usage
2. Set to "Use less data"
3. This reduces autoplay engagement

**Step 8: Make It Boring**
- Use in grayscale (phone setting)
- Only check at set times
- Remove from home screen

### TikTok Safety Guide (7 Steps)

**Step 1: Enable Screen Time Limits**
1. Settings → Digital Wellbeing
2. Daily screen time limit: 30-60 min
3. Set a passcode (give to accountability partner)

**Step 2: Disable Notifications**
1. Settings → Notifications
2. Turn OFF everything except DMs
3. Especially turn off: Suggested videos, trending

**Step 3: Refresh Algorithm**
1. Settings → Content preferences
2. "Refresh your For You feed"
3. Start fresh without addictive patterns

**Step 4: Clear History Weekly**
1. Settings → Watch history
2. Clear all watch history
3. Prevents algorithm from getting too good at hooking you

**Step 5: Restrict Browsing Time**
1. Digital Wellbeing → Restricted mode
2. Reduces addictive content types
3. Use a passcode you don't know

**Step 6: Disable Data-Saving Mode (Counterintuitive)**
1. Actually enable data-saving mode
2. Lower video quality = less engaging
3. Creates friction in viewing experience

**Step 7: Consider Deletion**
- TikTok is the most addictive platform
- If unable to control usage, delete it
- Use web version only if needed (more friction)

### Twitter/X Safety Guide (6 Steps)

**Step 1: Disable Timeline Algorithm**
1. Home → Star icon (top right)
2. Select "Following" instead of "For You"
3. Chronological = less addictive

**Step 2: Limit Notifications**
1. Settings → Notifications → Filters
2. Quality filter ON
3. Turn OFF: Recommendations, trending, highlights
4. Keep only: Mentions, DMs

**Step 3: Mute Trending**
1. Settings → Privacy and safety → Explore
2. Consider hiding "What's happening" entirely
3. Trending topics are designed to pull you in

**Step 4: Reduce Engagement Metrics**
1. Consider using third-party client
2. Some hide like/retweet counts
3. Reduces dopamine hits from metrics

**Step 5: Clean Following List**
1. Unfollow accounts that stress you out
2. Unfollow accounts that post too frequently
3. Keep only genuine value-add follows

**Step 6: Set Time Limits**
1. Use Alex Friend screen time
2. Set 20-30 minute daily limit
3. Twitter is a time sink

### YouTube Safety Guide (6 Steps)

**Step 1: Disable Autoplay**
1. Settings → Autoplay
2. Turn OFF autoplay on Home and Watch Next
3. This is the #1 rabbit hole creator

**Step 2: Limit Notifications**
1. Settings → Notifications
2. Turn OFF: Recommended videos, updates
3. Keep only: Specific channel notifications

**Step 3: Use Subscriptions Only**
1. Bookmark /feed/subscriptions URL
2. Always start there, never Home
3. Avoids algorithmic recommendations

**Step 4: Clear History**
1. Settings → History and privacy
2. Clear watch and search history
3. Consider pausing history permanently

**Step 5: Hide Shorts**
1. On any Short, tap "Not interested"
2. Consistently hide Shorts to reduce them
3. Consider using browser extension to hide Shorts

**Step 6: Use Distraction-Free Extensions**
1. Install "DF YouTube" or similar
2. Hides comments, related videos, end screens
3. Makes YouTube less engaging

### Snapchat Safety Guide (5 Steps)

**Step 1: Disable Streak Notifications**
1. Settings → Notifications
2. Turn OFF streak reminders
3. Streaks are designed to create obligation

**Step 2: Hide Discover/Spotlight**
1. Minimize time in Discover section
2. Stay in Chat tab only
3. Consider Discover a trigger zone

**Step 3: Limit Story Viewing**
1. Don't feel obligated to view all stories
2. Mute accounts with boring/triggering stories
3. Your time > "keeping up"

**Step 4: Review Friend List**
1. Remove people you don't actually talk to
2. Keep genuine connections only
3. Reduce obligation to maintain streaks

**Step 5: Set Usage Boundaries**
1. Only check at specific times
2. Remove from home screen
3. Use screen time limits

### Facebook Safety Guide (5 Steps)

**Step 1: Reduce News Feed**
1. Click "See less" on triggering content
2. Unfollow (don't unfriend) most people
3. Consider "Favorites" for close friends only

**Step 2: Disable Notifications**
1. Settings → Notifications
2. Turn OFF everything possible
3. Check manually instead of reactively

**Step 3: Hide Engagement Metrics**
1. Use browser extension if available
2. Reduces comparison and validation seeking
3. News Feed Eradicator can help

**Step 4: Leave Groups That Waste Time**
1. Review group memberships
2. Leave groups that don't add value
3. Groups can be major time sinks

**Step 5: Consider Deletion**
- Facebook often adds more stress than value
- Consider using Messenger only
- Delete main app, use web for rare checks

---

### Reddit Safety Guide (5 Steps)

**Step 1: Curate Subscriptions**
1. Leave subreddits that waste time
2. Keep only genuinely valuable communities
3. Avoid "entertainment" subreddits

**Step 2: Disable Recommendations**
1. Settings → Personalization preferences
2. Turn OFF recommendation features
3. Stick to subscribed subreddits only

**Step 3: Avoid r/all and r/popular**
1. These are infinite scroll traps
2. Use home feed only
3. Bookmark specific subreddits

**Step 4: Use Old Reddit**
1. old.reddit.com is less engaging
2. No infinite scroll
3. Cleaner, faster interface

**Step 5: Set Strict Time Limits**
1. Reddit is a major time sink
2. Set 15-20 minute limit
3. Use friction mode for Reddit app

## General Addiction-Breaking Strategies

### Reduce Phone Pickup Habits

**Remove Easy Access**
- Move apps off home screen
- Put in folders
- Use screen time limits with hard blocks

**Add Friction**
- Use Alex Friend friction mode
- Require intention to open
- Add delays before apps open

**Replace the Habit**
- When you reach for phone, do something else
- Keep alternatives ready (book, water, stretch)
- Notice the urge without acting

### Notification Detox

**Immediate Actions**
1. Disable ALL non-essential notifications
2. Keep only: calls, texts from real friends, calendar
3. Everything else can wait until you choose to check

**Notification Audit**
1. Go through every app
2. Ask: "Does this need to interrupt me?"
3. Default answer should be NO

### Grayscale Mode

**Why It Works**
- Colors are designed to grab attention
- Grayscale makes apps less appealing
- Reduces dopamine from viewing

**How to Enable**
- iOS: Settings → Accessibility → Color Filters → Grayscale
- Android: Developer options or Digital Wellbeing → Bedtime mode
- Consider enabling always or after certain hour

## Priority Order

For social media addiction, prioritize:

1. **TikTok** - Most addictive, consider deleting
2. **Instagram** - Comparison + infinite scroll
3. **YouTube** - Autoplay and rabbit holes
4. **Twitter/X** - Outrage + infinite scroll
5. **Reddit** - Endless subreddits
6. **Snapchat** - Streak pressure
7. **Facebook** - Time sink

### Daily Actions
- Honor time limits
- Don't override screen time
- Notice urges without acting

### Weekly Actions
- Review usage statistics
- Celebrate reductions
- Adjust limits as needed

### Monthly Actions
- Re-evaluate which apps you need
- Consider deleting low-value apps
- Reset algorithm if recommendations creep back
