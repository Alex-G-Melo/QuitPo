## Module 1: The Science of Digital Addiction
**Duration**: ~45 minutes (8 lessons)

### Lesson 1: What is Digital Addiction?
- Behavioral addiction in the digital age
- When phone use becomes compulsive
- The addiction cycle with devices
- Signs you're addicted (not just "using a lot")

**Key Insight**: If you can't stop when you want to, it's not just a habit.

**Journal Prompt**: "When did you first realize your phone use might be a problem?"

### Lesson 2: The Dopamine System
- How dopamine drives phone checking
- Variable rewards (notifications, likes)
- Why checking feels urgent
- The "seeking" behavior loop

**Key Insight**: Your phone delivers dopamine hits designed to be checked constantly.

**Journal Prompt**: "How many times a day do you check your phone without a reason?"

### Lesson 3: The Attention Economy
- Your attention is the product
- How social media makes money
- Engagement = addiction by design
- The billion-dollar industry capturing your focus

**Key Insight**: The smartest minds in tech are paid to make apps addictive.

### Lesson 4: Algorithm Manipulation
- How feeds keep you scrolling
- The outrage and emotion cycle
- Personalized content = personalized addiction
- Why you see what you see

**Key Insight**: The algorithm knows you better than you know yourself.

### Lesson 5: FOMO & Social Comparison
- Fear of Missing Out explained
- Highlight reels vs. real life
- Comparison as "joy thief"
- How FOMO keeps you checking

**Key Insight**: What you're "missing" is curated fiction.

**Journal Prompt**: "How does social comparison affect your mood?"

### Lesson 6: Attention Fragmentation
- What constant interruption does to focus
- The switch cost of multitasking
- Deep work vs. scattered attention
- Attention span decline research

**Key Insight**: Every notification costs you 20+ minutes of focus.

### Lesson 7: Physical & Mental Effects
- Sleep disruption from blue light and stimulation
- Anxiety and depression links
- Neck and posture problems
- Eye strain and headaches

**Journal Prompt**: "How has phone use affected your sleep and body?"

### Lesson 8: Quiz
8 questions testing knowledge from Module 1.

## Module 2: Your Brain on Presence
**Duration**: ~35 minutes (6 lessons)

### Lesson 1: The Recovery Timeline
- Attention span recovery science
- The 90-day reset
- What to expect each week
- Rebuilding focus capacity

**Key Insight**: Your attention span can heal. It takes time and practice.

### Lesson 2: Weeks 1-2: Withdrawal
- Phantom vibrations
- FOMO at its peak
- Boredom intolerance
- The urge to fill silence

**Journal Prompt**: "How will you handle FOMO in the first two weeks?"

### Lesson 3: Weeks 3-4: Finding Stillness
- Boredom becomes tolerable
- First signs of focus returning
- Presence in conversations
- Rediscovering offline pleasures

**Key Insight**: Boredom is actually your brain resetting to normal.

### Lesson 4: Month 2: Attention Strengthening
- Deep focus becoming possible
- Reading for longer periods
- Conversations without checking
- The phone becoming less magnetic

**Journal Prompt**: "What activities require focus that you want to reclaim?"

### Lesson 5: Month 3: Digital Minimalism
- Phone as tool, not master
- Intentional use patterns
- Boundaries feel natural
- FOMO rarely triggers

## Module 3: Relapse Prevention
**Duration**: ~40 minutes (7 lessons)

### Lesson 1: Understanding Digital Relapse
- Relapse as gradual return to old patterns
- Warning signs: increasing screen time
- "Just checking" becomes hours lost
- Notification creep

**Key Insight**: Relapse often starts with "just one quick check."

### Lesson 2: Environmental Controls
- Phone placement (not bedroom, not pocket)
- App limits and screen time settings
- Notification management
- Grayscale mode
- Physical barriers to access

**Journal Prompt**: "What environmental barriers will you set up?"

### Lesson 3: Coping Skills Toolkit
- Urge surfing for FOMO
- The "10 minute wait" technique
- Activity replacement list
- Mindfulness for presence

### Lesson 4: High-Risk Situation Planning
- Morning phone check habit
- Boredom and waiting time
- Emotional distress scrolling
- Nighttime in bed

**Journal Prompt**: "Write your morning phone-free routine."

### Lesson 5: Managing Slip-Ups
- One scroll session isn't total failure
- Recognize, stop, reset
- No shame spiral
- Learn from the slip

**Key Insight**: The goal is progress, not perfection.

### Lesson 6: Long-Term Digital Wellness
- Sustainable phone relationship
- Ongoing boundary maintenance
- Regular digital detox practices
- Life built around presence, not screens

### Lesson 7: Quiz
7 questions testing knowledge from Module 3.

## Module 4: Building Real Connection
**Duration**: ~45 minutes (7 lessons)

### Lesson 1: Social Media's Impact on Relationships
- Shallow connection vs. deep friendship
- The illusion of staying in touch
- Presence erosion in real relationships
- Comparison damaging self-worth

**Key Insight**: 1000 followers doesn't equal one real friend.

### Lesson 2: Reclaiming In-Person Connection
- Face-to-face vs. text communication
- Active listening without phone distraction
- Quality time without documentation
- Being fully present

**Journal Prompt**: "Who do you want to be more present with?"

### Lesson 3: Rebuilding Attention for Relationships
- Phone-free zones and times with others
- Eye contact and body language
- Deeper conversations
- Shared experiences (not photographed)

### Lesson 4: Managing FOMO in Social Settings
- Being at the event you're at
- Not documenting everything
- Missing some things is okay
- Quality of experience vs. fear of missing

**Key Insight**: The best moments are lived, not posted.

### Lesson 5: Healthy Digital Communication
- When texting is appropriate
- Video calls vs. text for important things
- Not substituting digital for real
- Digital boundaries in relationships

### Lesson 6: Finding Offline Community
- Hobbies with real people
- Clubs, groups, classes
- Volunteering and service
- Building local connections

**Journal Prompt**: "What offline community will you join?"

### Lesson 7: Quiz
7 questions testing knowledge from Module 4.
