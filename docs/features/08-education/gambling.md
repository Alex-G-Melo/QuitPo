## Module 1: The Science of Gambling Addiction
**Duration**: ~45 minutes (8 lessons)

### Lesson 1: What is Gambling Addiction?
- Behavioral addiction explained
- Pathological gambling criteria
- The gambling addiction cycle
- Why gambling is highly addictive

**Key Insight**: Gambling addiction is a brain disorder, not a character flaw.

**Journal Prompt**: "When did you first realize gambling might be a problem for you?"

### Lesson 2: The Dopamine System
- How dopamine drives behavior
- Variable reward schedules (the most addictive kind)
- Near-misses and their brain effects
- Why wins feel better than they should

**Key Insight**: Casinos are designed by neuroscientists to exploit your brain.

**Journal Prompt**: "How do near-misses affect your urge to continue gambling?"

### Lesson 3: The Gambler's Fallacy
- "I'm due for a win" thinking
- Independent probability explained
- Hot and cold streaks are illusions
- The house edge always wins long-term

**Key Insight**: Past results never influence future outcomes. Every bet is independent.

### Lesson 4: Chasing Losses
- The psychology of loss aversion
- Why we gamble more when losing
- The sunk cost fallacy
- How chasing guarantees greater loss

**Key Insight**: Chasing losses is how small problems become catastrophic.

**Journal Prompt**: "Describe a time when chasing losses made things worse."

### Lesson 5: Triggers & Cues
- Payday as trigger
- Sports events as triggers
- Gambling ads and promotions
- Emotional triggers (stress, boredom, excitement)
- HALT check

**Key Insight**: Knowing your triggers is the first step to defeating them.

**Journal Prompt**: "What are YOUR top 3 gambling triggers?"

### Lesson 6: The Casino's Tricks
- No clocks, no windows design
- Free drinks and rewards programs
- "Free bet" promotions (they're not free)
- Near-miss programming in slots
- Sound and light conditioning

**Key Insight**: Every casino feature is designed to make you lose more.

### Lesson 7: Financial & Emotional Toll
- The true cost of gambling (time, money, relationships)
- Shame, secrecy, and lies
- Impact on family and work
- The debt spiral

**Journal Prompt**: "What has gambling truly cost you beyond money?"

### Lesson 8: Quiz
8 questions testing knowledge from Module 1.

## Module 3: Relapse Prevention
**Duration**: ~40 minutes (7 lessons)

### Lesson 1: Understanding Gambling Relapse
- Relapse as process, not event
- The relapse chain for gamblers
- Warning signs: fantasizing about wins
- "Just one bet" thinking

**Key Insight**: The thought "just one bet" is the first domino.

### Lesson 2: Financial Barriers
- Self-exclusion from casinos (how to do it)
- Betting app blocks and deletion
- Giving up financial control temporarily
- Joint accounts and trusted person access

**Journal Prompt**: "What financial barriers will you put in place?"

### Lesson 3: Coping Skills Toolkit
- Urge surfing for gambling urges
- Delay techniques (24-hour rule)
- Think through the whole story (win is only part 1)
- Alternative excitement sources

### Lesson 4: High-Risk Situation Planning
- Payday survival plan
- Sports event strategies
- Casino proximity planning
- Friend/group gambling situations

**Journal Prompt**: "Write your payday survival plan."

### Lesson 5: Managing Slip-Ups
- One bet doesn't mean total relapse
- Stop immediately and leave
- Don't chase to "win back"
- Get support within 24 hours

**Key Insight**: A slip becomes a relapse when you keep going.

### Lesson 6: Long-Term Maintenance
- Lifestyle changes for lasting recovery
- Ongoing support (GA, therapy)
- New sources of excitement
- Financial goals to protect

### Lesson 7: Quiz
7 questions testing knowledge from Module 3.

## Sample Quiz Questions

### Module 1 Questions

**Q1**: Variable reward schedules (like in gambling) are:
- Less addictive than fixed schedules
- Not related to addiction
- **More addictive than fixed schedules** ✓
- Only found in slot machines

**Explanation**: Variable (unpredictable) rewards create the strongest dopamine response and are the most addictive.

**Q2**: The "Gambler's Fallacy" is the belief that:
- Gambling always leads to wins
- **Past results influence future outcomes** ✓
- Casinos are fair
- You can predict random events

**Q3**: "Chasing losses" typically results in:
- Winning back the money
- Breaking even
- **Losing even more money** ✓
- Neutral outcomes

### Module 2 Questions

**Q1**: During the first weeks of gambling recovery, you should expect:
- No cravings at all
- **Strong cravings during trigger events** ✓
- Immediate financial stability
- Boredom and restlessness
